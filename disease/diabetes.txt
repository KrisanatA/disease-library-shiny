<h4><b>Definition:</b></h4>

<p>Diabetes is a chronic disease that occurs either when the pancreas does not produce enough insulin or when the body cannot effectively use the insulin it produces. There are three types of diabetes:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Type 1 diabetes:</li>
    <ul>
      <li>Characterized by deficient insulin production and requires daily administration of insulin.</li>
    </ul>
  <li>Type 2 diabetes:</li>
    <ul>
      <li>Characterized by the bodyâ€™s ineffective use of insulin.</li>
    </ul>
  <li>Gestational diabetes:</li>
    <ul>
      <li>Hyperglycemia with blood glucose values is above normal but below those diagnostic of diabetes (occurs during pregnancy).</li>
    </ul>
</ul>

<h4><b>Disease category:</b></h4>

<p>NCD (Non-Communicable Disease)</p>

<h4><b>Risk factors:</b></h4>

<p>For Type 1, the cause are not exactly known</p>
<p>For Type 2:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Excess body weight</li>
  <li>Physical inactivity</li>
</ul>

<h4><b>Symptoms:</b></h4>

<ul>
  <li>Excessive excretion of urine</li>
  <li>Thirst</li>
  <li>Weight loss</li>
  <li>Vision changes</li>
</ul>
